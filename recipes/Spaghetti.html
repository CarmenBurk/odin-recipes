Homemade Spaghetti with Ground Beef

Welcome Home

A simple, slow cooked, authentic spaghetti sauce, as good as the restaurants serve...or better! TMom's secret ingredient is baking soda, but do not taste the sauce right after adding it. Wait a while, and this sauce will not disappoint you. It is good right from the stove, but the flavors blend overnight. Best served with your favorite meatball recipe, where the meatballs cook in the sauce the last half hour. Be prepared to take a nap after eating.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 (28 ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 (16 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Â½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 pound dried spaghetti noodles

Steps

  1. Bring 4 Quarts water to a rolling boil
  2. Add salt to water
  3. Boil Pasta until Al Dente
  4. Drain while saving 1 cup of pasta water
  5. Make the Pasta Sauce starting with Mixing together whole tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, water, mushrooms, onions, garlic, sugar, salt and pepper, basil, and 1 pinch of baking soda in a large saucepan
  6. Bring to a boil, stirring. Reduce to a simmer, and cook for 4 hours minimum.
  7. Stir in another pinch of baking soda; the sauce will foam. Simmer, stirring occasionally, until thick and almost brown. Make sure to scrape the sides of the pan in to the sauce.
  8. After the sauce is fork consistency, stir in Parmesan cheese
  9. Cool, cover, and refrigerate overnight. The next day, reheat and serve.
